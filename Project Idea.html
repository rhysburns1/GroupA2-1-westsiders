Project Idea

Melissa Eder 

Autonomous Community BUS 

The project we have in mind autonomous community bus.  Our idea is to have a community bus, like the buses used for public transport Victoria.   These community buses will have suspension that can lower itself and has an extendable ramp for scooters and wheelchairs.

People would be able to use PC or mobile phone app or dial up and book this bus for themselves.  A 22-seat bus would be a good size or could use the standard public transport bus (Remark Paringa Council, 2019).  The bus would also have an ability to be remotely controlled.  The bus would also have interactive with speakers and microphone so that passengers could ask any questions and hear any needed instructions.

When using the app or phoning to book a bus, the system will find the community bus nearest to the person that is free or available to take them and use a combination of google maps and VicRoads traffic to find the person and then making the bus go to that address (Marshall, 2017).

Therefore, the system will require that the autonomous buses constantly report where they are, their status, as in transit, who is on them and where they are going (Transdev Australasia, 2019). At first at least I think humans will have to have a high level of input, even if it’s using the microphones and speakers to interact with the passengers.  Once in transit the bus would take the person to their destination.  At first destination would have to be in a set community area, such as shops, post office, banks and so forth.

The buses as well as having suspension that can lower it to street level and an automatic extending ramp, would have to be equipped with a full array of sensors, forward, back, sides, above and under, with automatic sensing of other vehicles (Nott, 2019). The vehicles would be clearly marked and a certain colour, say green to let everyone know they are autonomous vehicles.  An advertising campaign letting people know that these buses are now on the roads, how to order one and so forth would also be done, to help inform the community as well as a dedicated web site with detailed information on the service (Nott, 2019).

We envision that these community buses be able to be remotely controlled, and obviously the buses would operate over encrypted channels to try and avoid hacking or the system.  We envision this being a free service, with funding to be provided by a combination of local, state and federal.  A fleet of 20 to 30 to start with, with that being built up, with the aim of having in the hundreds if not the 1000’s eventually.

These buses will also have to make decisions based on the safety of its uses, others on the road and the general community.  We see the following hierarchy of needs in our systems:

1.  The safety of bus passengers would be highest priority

2. Next pedestrians have next level of priority.  One might think that pedestrians might be the highest, but we feel that the safety of the people on the bus must be higher as otherwise people will not use the system

3. Other vehicles

4. The general community and the public best interest. 

Also once operating, these buses will come up with situations that we might need to know how to solve. For instance, what occurs if one is in an accident (Marshall, 2017)?  Our policy would be that unless the bus is mechanically or otherwise not able to function, it should finish its trip then return to base for any repairs or service.  This would cause issues with insurance, and this is where having cameras recording information, it would be able to determine if the accident was the fault of the autonomous vehicle or other vehicles. Obviously the buses wold have to have extended insurance to cover any issues should they occur (Marshall, 2017). Having the buses clearly marked and numbered will also help in the claims of any accident damage.

Another issue that might come up is a medical emergency of someone on board.  If someone were to have a medical emergency, then emergency services need to be informed and diverted to the bus so the person can get medical care.  Also, if we are to use 22 seat buses, then only a few wheelchairs or scooters will fill it, so when booking or using the app, passengers would need to stipulate if they are using wheelchairs, scooters or other walking assistance technologies.

The buses would also require fuel, most likely diesel or battery operated, with that being monitored, to ensure that the fuel tank or battery is always at least half full. Also, the vehicles will require cleaning and general maintenance, which would occur at the end of day in specialized workshops.

The aim would not be to replace the public transport Victoria system, but to complement it (Remark Paringa Council, 2019).  Also, after community buses, community cars, would be another goal of our organization. To have like an automated uber system run by us for the same reasons as a community bus.

So android, iPhone app, PC app, ability to call in and book via web site and so on are all vital. There is an initial outlay, but after that they system will save money, provide a much-needed service, and improve the lives of the elderly and disabled (Transdev Australasia, 2019).
